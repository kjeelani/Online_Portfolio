    Endless Woodlands

    This is a game that I use countlessly to entice my students into learning Python. It is a text-based adventure game where you are lost in an endless forest with no clue how you arrived. There are monsters to fight,puzzles to solve, and different boss encounters depending on your choices.     Snake Game

    A very basic two-player snake game that I plan on implementing into my curriculum in order to teach the turtle library. There are no textures, just shapes, but the functionality of the game works pretty well. There are two different types of "foods" that the snake can eat to gain length and speed. In the future, I might go back and create an algorithm that factors in the amount of food eaten and not just who dies first, but for now, not eating food seems to be the best way to win.     Demon vs. Hero

    It's in the name! This is a two player Javascript canvas game that has many interesting features. The first is that the hero is smaller/faster than the hero and can shoot swords. The hero can also be healed with Toxic Waste(I was 13 when I made this, don't judge). The demon, on the other hand, is slow and big, and takes a lot of damage from th Toxic Waste, but deals HUGE damage to the hero if the hero comes in contact with him. Make sure to have a friend to play with and have fun!
